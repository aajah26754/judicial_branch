Requirements

  • There are no official requirements for:
  • Age
  • Citizenship
  • Legal Experience
  • Education (not even a law degree)

The Appointment Process

  • Nomination - The President picks a candidate
  • Senate Judiciary Committee - The Senate reviews the candidate's background and hold hearings
  • Committee Vote - The Committee votes to recommend the candidate
  • Senate Vote - The full Senate debates and votes to confirm a candidate
  • Appointment - If it is confirmed, the candidate takes the oath of office

Sumpreme Court Nominees Usually Have

  • Legal Experience - Most justices have served as judges, attorneys, law professors, or government officials
  • A Law Degree - A law degree, normally from a prestigious law school
  • Judicial Experience - Solving problems between people, companies, and different government porblems
  • Senate Approval - They must still be confirmed by a majority vote in the Senate
