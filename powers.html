What are the Powers of the Judicial Branch?

The judicial branch decides if federal laws are constitutional and solves other problems about federal laws. The judges depend on the US government's executive branch to enforce court decisions.

The Primary Functions of the Judicial Branch

  • Judicial Review - The ability to interpret laws and determine if they are constitutional or not
  • Interpreting Laws - Decides how laws should be used in different cases
  • Settling Disputes - Solving problems between people, companies, and different government porblems
  • Ensuring Due Process - This protects individual rights and ensures legal processes
  • Checks Other Branches - Declares executive actions or legistlative laws unconstitutional
  • Handles Appeals - Reviews decisions made by other lower courts
