Brown v. Board of Education (1954) - Ending School Segregation

Brown v. Board of Education was a US Supreme Court case that challenged racial segregation in public schools.

    The Precedent Set
  • This cases overturned Plessy v. Ferguson (1896), this kept racial segregation under the "seperate but equal" doctrine
  • The Supreme Court ruled that racial segregation in public schools violated the Equal Protection Clause of the 14th Amendment
  • The court delcared that segregation is unconstitutional, this sets the stage for the Civil Rights Movement

    Changes Over Time
  • This decision had a lot of resistance, mainly in the Southern States, leading to slow changes
  • Brown II ordered schools to desegregate with "all deliberate speed," but there was weak enforcement
  • The Civil Rights Act of 1964 and other court ruling helped strengthened the decision and made integrating schools a national priority.

Department of Education v. Louisiana (2024)

The Background

In 2024, the US Department of Education changed the rules to help protect students. This protected them from discrimintion based on gender idenity and sexual orientation. Louisiana, Mississippi, Montana, and Idaho, challenged this rule. they argued that it unlawfully redefined sex discrimintion and overstepped students' and employees' rights.

Court Ruling

In June 2024, the US District Court for the Western District of Lousisana temporarily blcoked the enforcement of the Department's rule in plaintiff states. The courts ruled in favor of the states. the judge beleived that the Department may have went beyond its legal power.

The Impact

The court's decision stopped the new rule from being used in Lousisana and other states. This kept the same rules about discrimintion in pace. This case shows the ongoing dispute about how to define discrimintion and whether the federal government or states should make school policies.
